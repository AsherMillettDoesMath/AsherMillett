About Me

Hey my name is Asher, im a 3rd year undergraduate student in mathematical physics at Dalhousie university studying the mathematical basis of the standard model. My interests are far reaching, all the way from the gauge theories of Yang-Mills Ever to the differential geometric invariants of 4 manifolds created by Seiberg-Witten and Donaldson. My interests mathematically centralize on 4D manifold topology and geometry, aswell as the algebraic structures which underlie quantum field theory and string theory such as spectral theory and functional analysis. I take a hollistic approach to mathematics, tending to believe that it is all of a piece, and that no bit of mathematics is ever really isolated from any other. I am always looking for unexpected inter-field connections, specifically between algebra and geometry, aswell as algebra and topology. My current research hopes to derive some topological properties of smooth sections of principal G bundles valued in SU(n) in the large n limit, potentially using spectral theories to deal formally with the infinite dimensional case. On a more personal level, since I was very young I have been profoundly motivated to make, and so I have an enormous amount of productions which over the years ive managed too accumulate. My modern career direction Is entirely directed toward mathematics, however I decided when making this site that it would be good for any future employer/ buisness partners too see my other hobbies and interests. So I have created this website as a CV/gallery/library for all the random projects which ive happened to make in my life so far. I hope you enjoy browsing my lifes work. Updates come out whenever I can find the time, mathematical ones are always prompt and current since they are of proffesional importance. My philosophical work is hardly academically rigorous, however the considerations from analytic philosophy have certainly impacted on my problem solving and my overall perspective of what mathematics really is. Mathematics is, after all a powerful tool in the hand of a philosopher, so my inclination to explore these domaines overpowered my desire to stay out of the buisness of other fields. That work focuses mostly on the concepts of thinking as such, and how we can understand mathematics language and the unspeakable. I differ to philosophers like Nieztche, Heiddeger, Deleuze, Wittegenstein among other classical philosophers from western analytic and continental canons. They are certainly revealing as to my philosophy of mathematics and how I conduct and direct my research aswell as my more surface level thinking about mundane affaires. This website is who I am, it is all I have produced, so good luck abnd have fun exploring. If you want to contact me dont hesitate, im always looking for collaborators in mathematics arts and philosophy, so dont be a stranger, and maybe we can all learn together.
